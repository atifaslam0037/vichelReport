What is trim and why is it important? 

Many vehicle manufacturers make different versions of their models. These versions are often identified by letters (or a combination of letters). Examples are L for Luxury, S for Sport, X for off-road, G for an extended wheelbase and T for Turbo etc. These versions, or trims, can have different a different chassis, body-kit, engine, gearbox, suspension, interior finishing, and onboard equipment. These differences can have a considerable influence on the real market value of your vehicle, so it’s important to confirm which trim you are buying or selling. 

How do I know the trim (or version)? 

Most of the time, we can find the trim based on the VIN number you provided to us. If we cannot identify it, then you may find the trim letters on the vehicle (usually somewhere on the rear). You may also find it in the owner’s manual or on the original sales invoice. You can also often identify the trim if you know the vehicle’s specifics, like engine etc. If you are still unable to confirm the trim, then ask a specialized mechanic to help you.
